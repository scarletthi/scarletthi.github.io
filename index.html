History: Myanmar's history can be traced back to the 2nd century when the Rakhine ruled the west cost and Pyu civilization flourised in the central regions. Throughout the years great kingdoms came and went until the third Myamar Empire fell in 1885 to the colonial British; Myanmar subsenquently regained independence in 1948. For many years Myanmar disappeared behine a wall of self-isolation and only recently reopened its door to the outside world, revealing the country's unique culture and stunning scenery to new generations of visitors.

Places You Should Visit!!!

  1. The Shwedagon Pagoda

    Shwedagon Pagoda is one of the most famous pagodas in world and it is certainly the main attraction of Yangon, Myanmar. The temple is an important Buddhist monumet, with a long history that is surrounded by legend. It is also considered one of the olderst pagodaes in the world.

  2. The Bagan

    The Bagan is an ancient city in central Myanmar, southwest of Mandalay. Standing on the eastern banks of the Ayeyarwady River, it is known for Bagan Archaeological Area, where more than 2,000 Buddist monuments tower over green plains.

  3. The Inle Lake

    The Inle Lake is a major tourist attraction and this has led to some development of tourist infrastructure. Many small and large privately owned hotels and tour operations have arisen during the past few years. One of the tourst attractions in the village on lake itself is the traditional silversmithing. Houses in the village on the lake itself sit on stilts and are made of woven bamboo.

  4. The Mandalay Palace

    The Mandalay Palace is the last royal palace of the last Burmese monarchy. The palace was constructed between 1857 and 1859 as part of King Mindon's founding of the new royal capital city of Mandalay. The plan of Mandalay Palace largely follows the traditional Burmese palace design, inside a walled fort surrounded by a moat. Today, Mandalay Palace is a primary symbol of Mandalay and a major tourist destination.
